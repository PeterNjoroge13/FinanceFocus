Learn More About Building Credit

Building credit is an important aspect of personal finance, and there are many resources available online that provide guidance on this topic. Here are a few key points commonly covered.

Understand Credit Scores

Credit scores are numbers between 300 to 850, they are based on information found in an individual's credit report, which includes a history of borrowing and repayment behavior. It is used by lenders, such as banks and credit card companies, to evaluate the risk of lending money to a borrower.

Responsibly Use your Credit Card

  • Make On-Time Payments: Always pay at least the minimum amount due by the due date.
  • Consequences: lowers credit score which can affect your ability to get loans or even rent an apartment.
  • Keep Balances Low: Aim to use around 30% of your credit limit to maintain a good credit score.
  • Monitor Your Account: Regularly check your statements for errors, fraudulent charges, and your spending.

Starting to Build Credit

Get a Credit Card:

  • Authorized User: Become an authorized user on somebody else's credit card account. You'll have some access to the account, but the primary cardholder will be responsible for making payments.
  • Student Credit Cards: designed for students and often have lower credit limits and fewer perks. More likely to be more lenient in regards to credit history requirements.
  • Unsecured Credit Cards: These do not require a deposit and are based on your creditworthiness.
  • Secured Credit Cards: These require a security deposit, which serves as your credit limit. They are ideal for building or rebuilding credit.
